Program Overview
Program Educational Objectives

What graduates will achieve 3–5 years after graduation:

  1. Lead AI deployment engagements — from use case identification to production — at enterprises, consulting firms, or technology organizations of varying scale.
  2. Apply sound technical and organizational judgment to deployment decisions, bridging what AI can do and what organizations actually need done.
  3. Advance responsible AI deployment in organizational contexts, contributing to governance, accountability, and ethical practice in the field.
  4. Progress into senior roles — Forward Deployed Engineering lead, Chief AI Officer, AI Strategy director, or entrepreneurial positions — within five years of graduation.
Program Educational Outcomes

What students will be able to do upon graduation:

  1. Design and architect AI systems that connect frontier models to enterprise data, APIs, and organizational infrastructure.
  2. Execute the full AI deployment lifecycle — from problem framing through production deployment and impact measurement.
  3. Apply change management and stakeholder engagement skills to ensure AI deployments are adopted and sustained.
  4. Govern AI deployments responsibly, applying risk, compliance, and accountability frameworks appropriate to the context.
  5. Communicate AI system design, deployment decisions, and business impact to technical and non-technical audiences.
  6. Demonstrate professional practice through a year-long Build Studio engagement resulting in a deployed, production AI system for a real sponsor organization.
Intended Audience

The MS in AI Systems Deployment & Management is designed for professionals seeking roles at the intersection of AI technology and organizational transformation.

Particularly well-suited for:

  • Recent BS graduates in information systems, computer science, business analytics, or related fields seeking technical depth with organizational impact.
  • Early-career professionals (1–4 years) in IT, consulting, product management, or business operations repositioning for AI-focused roles.
  • Technical practitioners who want the organizational and strategic fluency to lead AI deployments — not just build them.

Basic programming fluency and quantitative reasoning are assumed. Prior AI experience is not required.
