About Summer 2026

Nine weeks. Four teams. Real learning.

Build Studio Summer 2026 is a hybrid program running June through August. Teams engage intensely on scheduled session days — working with Professor Lindberg and guest practitioners through structured workshops, industry talks, and hands-on building — with a public Demo Day to close out the summer.

Each project sits at the edge of what AI can do today. There are no templates. There is no safety net. Teams build what hasn't been built yet.

Summer 2026 Projects

Four projects at the frontier

Each team takes on a challenge that combines AI with real learning — machines that navigate, agents that reason, and stories that document what it means to grow up alongside AI.

🚁

Voice-Commanded Safety Drone

Occupational Safety & Health · Autonomous Systems

Most workplace safety inspections put humans in the danger zone. This project builds an autonomous quadcopter you can talk to — describe the hazard, the drone investigates. The AI system interprets natural-language commands, navigates the environment, and reports back what it finds. Built for the kinds of sites — construction zones, industrial facilities, confined spaces — where sending a human is the last resort.

🚗

Off-Road Autonomous Vehicle

Autonomous Navigation · Unstructured Terrain

Self-driving cars assume roads exist. This one doesn't. This project builds an AI-powered autonomous vehicle designed for terrain with no maps, no lanes, and no predictable surface — the kind of environment found in disaster response, agriculture, and off-grid operations. The challenge: train a system that can read the ground and find its own way through.

🔬

AI Research Agent

Computational Research · Theory Construction

Building theory is one of the least legible processes in academic research. This project builds an autonomous AI agent that works alongside researchers constructing computational theories — surfacing assumptions, flagging gaps, and making the reasoning process more transparent and reflexive. Not a search tool. Not a writing assistant. A thinking partner that helps researchers see what they can't see themselves.

🎬

The AI Generation

Student Documentary · AI & the Future

What does it actually feel like to grow up with AI? This team makes the documentary. Through interviews, observation, and first-person footage, they capture how young people today experience AI — not the headlines, not the hype, but the everyday reality of a generation that has never known a world without it. And how they imagine the future they're about to enter.

"The most important thing you can build this summer is your judgment about what's worth building." — Build Studio Summer 2026
