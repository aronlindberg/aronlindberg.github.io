About the Show

Two tracks. One conversation about building.

Every episode weaves together two kinds of content — the raw inside story of student teams building in real time, and broader conversations with the people shaping how things get built in the world.

🎬

The Documentary Track

Every semester, we follow real Build Studio student teams in real time — from the moment they receive their sponsor challenge to the final demo. Students produce video footage as a course requirement. That footage becomes the spine of the show.

  • Real teams, real challenges, real sponsor feedback
  • Student-produced footage — building is on camera; so are the pivots and failures
  • Sponsor advisors reveal challenges, check in at midpoint, and react to final demos
  • The hackathon: one dedicated episode of raw, high-pressure footage
  • Season arc mirrors the course: 3 acts, 13–14 episodes, demo day finale
🎙️

The Guest Conversation Track

Beyond the studio, we invite people who build things — founders, engineers, researchers, product leaders, innovators — to talk honestly about what building actually looks like. No polished retrospectives. The messy, real version.

  • Founders and builders: what you wish you'd known earlier
  • Engineers and CTOs: how AI is changing the way products get made
  • Researchers: what the science says about how people and AI create together
  • Product leaders and innovators: building inside large organizations
  • Educators: redesigning how the next generation learns to build
"A team can build something excellent and tell it badly. A team can struggle with the product but produce riveting footage. That tension is the show." — Build Studio: The Podcast

Episode Structure

What's in each episode

Every episode (~30–40 min) follows the same six-segment structure, mixing student-produced footage with host commentary and guest voices.

Segment Produced By What It Does
Cold Open Students
curated by hosts		 Drops the listener into a live moment of tension or surprise from the studio — no context, no framing. The hook.
The Frame Aron & Aleksi Introduces one sharp idea — from research, practice, or the news — that gives the episode its intellectual lens. Connects what the students are doing to something bigger.
Student Segment Students
+ host reaction		 Multi-team check-ins or a single-team deep dive, with real-time host commentary. The core of the documentary track: what's getting built, what's breaking, what's surprising.
Sponsor or Guest Voice Guest A sponsor advisor reacts to what the teams are doing — or an external guest (founder, engineer, researcher, innovator) brings a broader perspective on the episode's frame idea.
The Confessional Cut Students
rotating teams		 One team, unscripted, direct to camera. What are they not saying in their main footage? The most honest two minutes of the episode.
The Takeaway Aron & Aleksi Closes the loop on the frame idea. One clean insight. A tease of what's coming next week.
Season Structure

13–14 episodes. Three acts.

A full season maps to one semester of the Build Studio course, following the same arc from challenge reveal to demo day.

Act I
The Setup
Episodes 1–3
  • The cast is introduced — teams, sponsors, hosts
  • Ep 2: "The Brief" — sponsors reveal challenges on camera
  • Teams hear their challenge for the first time
  • First student-produced segments appear
Act II
The Grind
Episodes 4–10
  • Weekly build documentation — pivots, breakthroughs, failures
  • Ep 6: "Hackathon Live" — raw 1-day sprint footage
  • Ep 8: "Sponsor Checkpoint" — live midpoint reactions
  • Single-team feature episode
  • A meta episode on the production process itself
Act III
The Finish
Episodes 11–14
  • The final sprint — feature-complete or bust
  • Ep 12: "Demo Day" — artifacts presented, highlight reels screened
  • Ep 13: "The Verdict" — will any artifacts be adopted or funded?
  • Season retrospective + bonus outtakes
Season Themes

Each season has a lens

A thematic question that sharpens the frame for the whole season — connecting what student teams are doing to a broader conversation about AI and building.

Season Theme The Question
Season 1 First Contact Can AI actually build it? Learning what the tools can and cannot do.
Season 2 Build for Someone Specific Sponsors bring named users with real, documented pain. Does knowing the person change what you build?
Season 3 Accountability by Design Products must include explainability. Who is responsible when AI builds?
Season 4 Ship It Only artifacts that could go live earn full credit. What does it take to actually finish?

Why This Format

What makes it different

Students are co-producers, not subjects

They don't just appear in the podcast — they make it. Weekly video submissions are course requirements. Learning to tell the story of building is as important as building.

The drama is real

Sponsors bring genuine challenges. Feedback is honest. Pivots happen for real reasons. Nothing is staged. The tension comes from the work, not from manufactured conflict.

The intellectual frame elevates it

Aron and Aleksi connect what student teams are experiencing to broader ideas about AI, product development, and the future of knowledge work. The studio becomes a lens on the field.
