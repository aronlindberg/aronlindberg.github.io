For Companies

How to submit a challenge

A good challenge brief is short, specific, and grounded in a real business need. Here's what we're looking for.

🎯

A real business context

What does your organization do? What part of the business does this challenge relate to?

❓

A clear problem statement

What specific problem or opportunity would you like teams to address? What would success look like?

📊

Data or domain guidance

Can teams use public datasets? Do you have a preferred domain or industry context? (Proprietary data not required.)

💡

AI opportunity

Where do you see AI adding genuine value? What kind of system or tool would be most useful?

Example Challenge Brief

A retail bank receives thousands of customer inquiries through its chatbot about various issues, but the chatbot does not always correctly understand customer requests or generate insights about emerging customer needs. Design an AI-enabled system that improves how the chatbot interprets and responds to customer queries while generating useful insights for the bank. Teams may use datasets such as Banking77, which contains over 13,000 customer queries across 77 banking intents. The solution should include either a software artifact or a system proposal and a business case explaining how it could improve customer service, reduce support costs, or create new business opportunities.
