HEAD — Human-Embedded Agentic Design

The human designer holds the vision and guardrails. Design agents generate. Crit agents critique. Eval artifacts hand work back to a human who stays in charge. AI in the loop of the designer — not the reverse.

Conceptual, not tool-training
Tools show up — and they'll use them — but the workshop is about the conceptual moves: how learning, authorship, and judgment shift when you build with agents.

The work
4 sessions × 2h
Each session is a conceptual move, enacted by building. Click any session to expand.

Reframe a real training problem as a design challenge. Define the human gestalt that drives HEAD — the vision and guardrails the designer holds while agents do the generative work.

Build the context architecture — their training domain as material the agents draw on. Learning here means assembling what the system needs to know, not absorbing what an instructor delivers.

Run the design/crit agent loop. Generate learning solutions and push back on them — the crit agent surfaces weaknesses, the human decides. Judgment becomes the primary skill.

Use eval artifacts to finalize. Pitch the solution. Reflect on what learning has become — for ICE-T and for the field force they serve. The infrastructure built here is what they take back.
