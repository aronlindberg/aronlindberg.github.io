1 of 5 The question
What does learning look like in the world of AI?

AI is changing the way we learn. Asynchronous content can no longer be static — it increasingly needs to be interactive, and deeply integrated with live, in-person or virtual workshops that happen in real time.

This is a proposal for how we can rethink what learning looks like in the world of AI — and spread that new methodology across an organization through its learning and development team.

Overview
Who this is for
An organization that drives learning across a large, complex corporation.
You drive learning at scale

Not a tools class
This isn't about how to use Claude, or which buttons to click. The tools will show up — but they're not the point. Deploying AI without building judgment first creates significant organizational risk.Anderson et al., 2025
Rethinking learning itself
The real question is how we rethink what learning looks like across a complex organization when AI changes the ground rules.
Why this matters to L&D

You don't just learn — you design learning for thousands. Reconceiving it is strategic, not academic.

Human-embedded agentic design ↗

The human designer holds the vision and guardrails.Van Der Meulen & Wixom, 2024 Design agents generate. Crit agents critiqueRecker et al., 2023 — eval artifacts hand the work back to a human who stays in charge. AI in the loop of the designer — not the reverse.Raisch & Krakowski, 2021

Conceptual, not tool-training
Tools show up — and you'll use them — but the work is about the conceptual moves: how learning, authorship, and judgment shift when you build with agents. While the cost of AI capability is falling, the premium on human judgment is not.Kolbjørnsrud, 2023
